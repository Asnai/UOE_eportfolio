Collaborative Discussion 1 Summary

As automation enters the world of data science, more and more tasks currently being done by data scientists are being automated which has led to discussions about the future of data scientists. As of today, it still seems farfetched that the current roles of a data scientist will be outdated due to amount of data, analytics, and AI in business and society seem unlikely to decline, Davenport & Patil (2022).

Automation is already having a significant impact in the field of data science. As stated by Saxena (2021), in 2019, data scientists used to spend days in data gathering, data cleaning, feature selection, but now the same tasks can be accomplished faster by tools that are available in the market. Data scientists are having to adapt to the market. Jee (2021) states that in the future data scientists will spend more time focusing on tasks that automation struggles.

Data scientists will continue to be relevant for years to come, but will have to adapt as the years go by.

Regarding Master Data Management, there are many advantages of Master Data Management. A few advantages stated by Benjamin (2020) are:

  • it helps reduce workload since every piece of data is collected only once.
  • It helps to reduce time to market for new systems, as it can easily connect to the master data system.

There are also drawbacks to Master Data Management. Landgraf (2021) states that:

  • It is slow and complicated
  • the projects take forever to complete and have a high failure rate.

Overall, I believe that while Master Data Management was created to help departments set up a single source of truth, it is time to move on. Master Data Management was set up in a time when all the data is on the company’s premise which is not the case today. Master Data Management is too slow and complicated, and in today’s fast-moving market, businesses need to be quick and efficient, embrace distributed truth and democratize data. Landgraf (2021).
